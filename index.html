The Problem

  • Lack of Transparency & Regulation:

    The prop trading and brokerage space, especially in emerging markets, is rife with unregulated entities, leading to distrust and scams.

  • Underserved Retail Traders:

    Many retail traders, particularly in high-growth regions like the Gulf and Asia, lack access to legitimate, high-quality, and fair trading platforms.

  • High Barriers to Entry for Talent:

    Aspiring professional traders face significant capital requirements and limited opportunities to hone their skills in a structured, compliant environment.

  • Fragmented Ecosystem:

    The market is split between standalone brokers and prop firms, often lacking a cohesive ecosystem that supports traders from education to funded trading.

  • Geographical Restrictions:

    Limited access for traders in specific regions due to regulatory hurdles or lack of localized offerings from global players.

Business Model & Revenue Structure

Brokerage Revenue Streams

  • Spreads (tight, competitive)
  • Commissions (low-fee structure)
  • Swap Fees (overnight positions)
  • Premium Accounts (advanced tools, dedicated support)
  • API Access (for institutional/algorithmic traders)

Proprietary Trading Firm Revenue Streams

  • Evaluation Fees (for trading challenges)
  • Profit Splits (favorable for funded traders)
  • Challenge Fees (re-takes, resets)
  • Funded Account Management Fees (small percentage on capital)
  • Educational Content Sales (via Tradyom integration)

Combined Synergies: Cross-promotion, client migration between platforms, robust data analytics for both segments, enhanced brand loyalty through comprehensive offerings.
